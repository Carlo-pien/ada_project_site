Uncovering the Digital Transmission of COVID-19

A Data Story by TheDataDiggers22

We all followed the COVID-19 pandemic maps as it propagated around the world. However, how present was the pandemic mentally in different population around the World ? Embark with us on a quest to understand and link Wikipedia pageview statistics to real-world behavior of different populations during the pandemic.

Let's begin

Why Wikiedia?

Wikipedia is a unique information sharing platform with its open-source management style and open-collaboration editing system. As it has no marketing or advertising campaign, it is reasonable to assume that Wikipedia reflects the interest of the population: people look up Wikipedia when they want information on a particular subject. While Media directs attention, Google and other search engines show what currently has people’s attention, Wikipedia shows what the population wants to know more about (i.e. interest). Using this, we can access the “mental presence” of the virus across cultures and time and analyse how much people cared about the virus.

Dive into the data story

Introduction

In this datastory, we will examine the connection between the information-seeking behavior of people on Wikipedia and its relation to real-world actions during the COVID-19 pandemic. To do this, we have compiled a dataset containing Wikipedia pageview statistics for COVID-19-related articles in 40 different languages that are representative of various countries, such as Sweden. By comparing these pageview statistics to COVID-19 death rates in these countries, we can see how information-seeking behavior relates to the pandemic.

We will also use the Google mobility dataset and a trust dataset measuring people's trust in their government to visualize the complex relationship between information-seeking behavior, trust, and mobility during the COVID-19 period. Finally, we will delve deeper into select countries to get a more detailed understanding of these relationships.

 

Our Datasets: tl;dr version

Wikipedia Pageviews

Timeseries of pageviews of COVID-19 related articles by language and by day for the entire COVID-19 period.

Google Mobility

Google mobility dataset containing the evolution from baseline of mobility classified in 6 different categories.

Trust Dataset

Dataset representing the trust of the population towards government, journalists and science, by country.

COVID-19 cases and deaths

Number of cases and deaths per country and per day since the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic.

 

Our Datasets: engineer version

Wikipedia pageview statistics

To begin with, we had the full dataset of all COVID-related articles in 175 languages. Of course, this does not align with our research questions so we performed the following preprocessing steps:

  1. To narrow our focus, we eliminated any languages that had fewer than 10 articles and any languages that were not specific to a single country (such as English and Spanish). We determined that a language was specific to a country if at least 75% of the people who spoke it lived in that country. We treated each country as having a single dominant language. We validated this assumption after further investigation.

  2. After this step, we are left with 40 countries/languages, which represents 30.6% of the total world population. From there, extracting the pageview statistics form the 1st january 2020 to the 31 jully of 2022 was easy.
